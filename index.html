Get A Life!

Get A Life!

Project Description

Get A Life! is my first solo effort at game development. I spent a few months putting it all together, from the art assets to the music to the code, all of it came from my mind. I was very pleased with the outcome.

I developed Get A Life! in Game Maker as my final project for Game Development I. It is an action platformer where you play as a robot in a dystopian future.

The year is 20X6; humanity is on the brink of extinction from their robotic overlords. Courtney is a robot that wants something different out of life. She wants to become a human, but is unsure of how to start. Robots aren’t quite as good at biology as humans, so Courtney deduces that the only way to become human is to harvest the limbs of humans and attach them to herself. The remaining two leaders of the human resistance, ex-Basketball player Houlihan “The Legs” Kristopherson and Surgeon General Dr. Hans Händi, represent the last bastion of hope for the human race. As the finest specimens of human talent alive, Courtney begins her crusade to exterminate the two powerful generals of the Human Resistance Force.

App Development
Chord College

Chord College

Project Description

I designed Chord College in order to try and teach people some of the basic foundations of playing the guitar. Music is another of my hobbies, and I was recently tasked with teaching someone to play the guitar. I was having trouble finding free resources that would adequately teach them to play simple chords, so my frustration lead to this app.

It took a few weeks for me to learn the necessary skills to make this app, as it would integrate sound, images, and text in a nested table view. I was incredibly pleased with the outcome.

Filterize it!

Filterize it!

Project Description

Filterize it! is a simple app which utilizes the iPad's camera. It applies any of the five filters(Sepia, Vignette, Noir, Vintage, or Blur) to the photo of the user's choice.

This app was relatively simple to develop, and was more of a test to see what I could do with the camera functionality of the iPad.

App Development
Storm the Castle

Storm the Castle

Project Description

Storm the Castle is a Work in Progress game that I am creating for the iPad. It will feature gameplay mechanics from Breakout, a popular Atari game. Storm the Castle sets itself apart from the Atari classic, in that it utilizes the iPad's accelerometer to move the paddle.

The user will be tasked with destroying a number of medieval buildings in this game, and will compete against the clock to get the highest score possible.
